# Focus Space experience principles

This document distils the July 2026 vision pack into constraints for implementation.

1. Focus Space manages attention, not tasks.
2. X and Y express hierarchy and relationships; Z always expresses attention.
3. Objects are directly manipulable. There is no global edit mode.
4. Changes in state are communicated through calm, natural motion.
5. Camera changes animate; they never jump.
6. The workspace stays visually quiet, with depth and light carrying more meaning than chrome.
7. RealityKit is an adapter. Domain and application logic remain renderer-independent.
8. Persistence is automatic and human-readable.
9. Time may influence attention, but it does not turn the product into a conventional scheduler.
10. When correctness and beauty genuinely conflict, prefer the interaction people will enjoy returning to.

## Current experience slice

The first slice validates the central metaphor: arranging a hierarchy in X/Y while changing attention in Z. It includes direct selection, movement, renaming, contextual hierarchy actions, attention changes, filtering, search, history, and autosave.

Camera orbit/pan/frame-selected and time-driven attention remain subsequent milestones. They should be added only after the depth manipulation itself feels excellent.
